TRADITIONAL FOODS YOU MUST TRY IN

KOTA DEPOK

GABUS PUCUNG
Gabus Pucung is part of the Betawi culinary tradition, reflecting the rich culture of the local community. The name "pucung" refers to kluwek as the main ingredient for its broth.

This dish used to be very popular in areas around Jakarta and Depok because gabus (snakehead fish) was abundant in local waters. The black color of the broth comes from kluwek, the seed of the kepayang tree, which gives a unique, savory, and slightly earthy flavor.

Gabus Pucung is best enjoyed warm with white rice. This dish offers a distinctive taste that combines savory, slightly bitter, and deep spices, making it perfect for lovers of traditional Indonesian food.

If you have the opportunity, tasting Gabus Pucung in its place of origin will provide an authentic culinary experience!

DAGE
Dage is a traditional food commonly found in Depok. It is a simple preparation made from tofu, tempeh, or other fermented soybean products, fried in a seasoned flour batter. Dage is a popular snack in traditional markets because of its distinctive savory taste and affordable price.

The dage sold in Depok is often made from local tempeh bungkil (a by product of soybean processing), giving it a slightly richer flavor and denser texture compared to regular tempeh.

Its simple yet rich taste makes it a favorite among locals. Although simple, dage reflects the uniqueness of local cuisine, utilizing inexpensive yet delicious ingredients.

Its crispy exterior and soft interior make it suitable to enjoy anytime.

BIR PLETOK
Bir Pletok is a traditional Betawi beverage that is also well-known in surrounding areas such as Depok. Despite its name containing the word "beer," this drink contains no alcohol at all. Bir Pletok is a blend of spices known for its warming sensation, fragrant aroma, and health benefits.

The inspiration for this drink came from Dutch colonial influences that introduced the tradition of drinking beer. The local community then created their own healthier and alcohol-free version using traditional ingredients. The name "Pletok" is believed to come from the sound "pletok," which is heard when ice cubes are added to the drink, especially when served cold.

Bir Pletok is suitable for enjoyment in various settings, especially at night or in cold weather. This beverage is often served at traditional Betawi events such as weddings or celebrations and is a popular souvenir from Depok.
